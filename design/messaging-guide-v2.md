# Chasing a Cure PD — Messaging Guide v2

_Supersedes messaging-guide.md. This version retires unverifiable "100%" claims and replaces them with honest, specific language about how we operate._

---

## The core claim

> **Donations go directly to Parkinson's research through The Michael J. Fox Foundation and Team Fox.** We keep operating costs to the bare minimum required to run the foundation — event expenses, processing fees, and the tools we need to function. No one draws a salary. Everything else funds the science.

This is the sentence we stand behind. It's true, it's specific, and it doesn't require us to defend a number we can't prove to the penny.

---

## What we say — and what we no longer say

### Keep ✓
- "Volunteer-run" — no one takes a salary, factually accurate
- "No salaries. No paid staff."
- "Founder-led"
- "Directly to research through MJFF and Team Fox"
- "Research-first"

### Retire ✗
- "100% of every dollar to research"
- "100% to research"
- "Every penny goes to the cure"
- "Zero overhead"
- "No overhead"
- "Zero administrative cut"

We cannot defend these at the penny level (payment processing fees alone make them untrue), and a donor who reads the 990 will notice. Retire them everywhere.

---

## Approved replacement lines

Drop-in phrases, ranked from shortest to longest:

- *Volunteer-run. Research-first.*
- *No salaries. No paid staff.*
- *The founders don't draw a dime.*
- *We keep operations as lean as a foundation legally can.*
- *Your gift flows from Chasing a Cure directly to MJFF and Team Fox.*
- *We publish our 990. Ask us anything.*

---

## The three-step flow (replaces the "100%" stat tile)

Wherever we previously used a big "100%" number, use the flow instead:

**1. Your gift** — You donate to Chasing a Cure PD.
**2. Chasing a Cure** — We cover only what's required to operate: event costs, processing fees, basic tools. No salaries.
**3. Research** — The remainder flows directly to The Michael J. Fox Foundation and Team Fox, funding Parkinson's science.

This is more trust-building than any percentage because it names the pathway and the costs, rather than hiding them behind a round number.

---

## Tone

- **Confident, not boastful.** State what's true. Don't claim what can't be proven.
- **Specific over superlative.** "No one draws a salary" beats "100% efficient."
- **Transparent on request.** If a donor asks where a dollar went, we show them. The 990 is public.
- **Family-first, research-always.** Wendy's voice stays intimate. The research claim is the spine underneath it.

---

## Guardrails for future copy

Before publishing any new donor-facing line, check:

1. Does it contain a number we cannot verify at the penny? → rewrite.
2. Does it imply zero costs of any kind? → rewrite.
3. Does it name the pathway (MJFF / Team Fox)? → good.
4. Does it say or imply someone is paid? → only say "volunteer" / "no salaries."
5. Would we be comfortable if a journalist pulled our 990 and compared? → if yes, ship it.
