## 10. Risks and Mitigations

- **Risk:** Raffle revenue underperforms the $40,000 target. **Mitigation:** Tiered pricing ($100 / 1 ticket, $250 / 3 tickets, $500 / 10 tickets) to raise average order value while preserving an exclusive feel; guarantee a floor through pre-committed sponsor purchases before the grand opening; track weekly sell-through and boost paid promotion if pacing lags.
- **Risk:** Budget overrun on catering and rentals (easy to slip at a private-residence event). **Mitigation:** Lock fixed-price vendor contracts by week 4; keep a strict change-order protocol; pursue in-kind donations to replace cash line items.
- **Risk:** Weather on July 18 (Concert for a Cure is outdoor at the lake home in Indiana summer). **Mitigation:** Tent rental baseline in the budget; have an indoor-backup plan documented.
- **Risk:** Wendy and Rob as the solicitation bottleneck. **Mitigation:** Block calendar time week 1 for the top 25 list; use pre-drafted letter and script so outreach is fast, not bespoke.
- **Risk:** Indy Boat Co grand opening slips, compressing raffle window. **Mitigation:** Confirm opening date week 2; have a backup raffle push plan for the final 30 days if needed.
- **Risk:** Fund-a-need falls flat at the event. **Mitigation:** Seed the room with 2–3 pre-committed opening pledges at $1K+; tie the ask to a specific, nameable MJFF research milestone.

## 12. Boat Raffle Pricing and Plan

**Prize:** 2026 boat donated by Indy Boat Co, valued at $25,000
**Target revenue:** $40,000
**Positioning:** Exclusive $100 floor to match the Concert for a Cure ticket price point and the caliber of the room.
**Pricing structure:**

| Package | Price | Per-ticket cost | Value framing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 ticket | $100 | $100.00 | Entry / single-try |
| 3 tickets | $250 | $83.33 | Most popular |
| 10 tickets | $500 | $50.00 | Best value — serious contender |

**Estimated probability of hitting $40K:** ~70–80%
**Expected revenue range:** $38K–$58K
**Expected AOV:** $160–$200
**Expected order volume:** 200–275 orders (roughly 300–500 tickets in circulation)

**Exclusivity lever (recommended):** Cap total tickets sold at 500 or 750 and market the cap — *"Only 500 tickets will be sold"* is a proven urgency driver that also keeps winner odds meaningful (1:500 at a sellout).

**Channel plan for raffle sales:**
- In-store at Indy Boat Co (posters, QR code, printed tickets at checkout) — begins at their June grand opening
- Online raffle page (co-branded Chasing a Cure PD + Indy Boat Co), linked from all campaign emails and social posts
- Event-night sales on July 18 (cash, card, and QR code at the lake home through the raffle drawing)

**Key rules to display clearly on the raffle page and all collateral:**
- Eligibility per IGC license terms (confirm Indiana residency or other restrictions)
- Drawing date: Saturday, July 18, 2026, live at Concert for a Cure
- Winner need not be present to win
- Tax implications for the winner (prize is taxable income — disclose)
- Refund policy and alternate-prize terms if the primary prize becomes unavailable

**Soft floor strategy:** Secure $10,000–$15,000 in pre-committed raffle ticket purchases from 2–3 core sponsors before the grand opening (at $250 or $500 tier). This de-risks the target and creates visible momentum when public sales launch.

**Pacing checkpoints:**
- End of Phase 2 (Jun 7): 15% of target ($6K) from quiet-phase sponsor purchases
- End of grand opening weekend (Jun 14): 40–50% of target ($16K–$20K)
- 2 weeks out (Jul 5): 75% of target ($30K)
- Event night (Jul 18): Remaining 25% ($10K) from final push and event-night sales

If pacing falls behind at any checkpoint, boost paid social spend and add an email send that week.
