# Chasing a Cure PD — Top 25 Major Donor Outreach Campaign
## Quiet Phase: April 20 – May 17, 2026

**Goal:** 10–15 gifts at $1,000+ totaling $30,000 from personal major-donor prospects before the public campaign launch on May 18.

**Owners:** Wendy Chase and Rob Spalding — all outreach is personal and 1:1. No mass email. No form letters sent without personalization.

**Voice:** Deeply personal, direct ask, no filler. Write like Wendy is typing it at the kitchen table. *(See messaging-guide.md § 6 — Tone by channel: Major donor letter.)*

---

## How the Sequence Works

Each prospect moves through a 4-touch sequence over 3–4 weeks. Every touch has a single job.

| Touch | Method | Timing | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Touch 1** | Personalized letter or email | Week 1 (Apr 20–26) | Open the door — story, urgency, soft ask |
| **Touch 2** | Phone call | 3–5 days after letter | Have the real conversation, make the direct ask |
| **Touch 3** | Voicemail + follow-up email | If no answer on call | Keep the door open, make it easy to respond |
| **Touch 4** | Final follow-up | 7–10 days after call | Close or gracefully table for the event |

Every gift at this stage gets a personal thank-you from Wendy within 48 hours — phone call first, handwritten note within the week.

## Phone Call Script

Use this as a guide, not a script. Sound like yourself. The goal of the call is a conversation — not a recitation.

### Before you dial
- Know which bucket this prospect is in (A, B, or C)
- Have the ask amount written in front of you — do not improvise it on the call
- Know one specific MJFF research update you can mention if asked (check michaeljfox.org for a current milestone)
- Have the Concert for a Cure date (July 18) and location (lake home, Fishers) ready

---

### Opening (30 seconds)

> "Hi [First name], it's Wendy. Do you have a few minutes?"

Wait. Let them respond. If they say they're busy:
> "Absolutely — when would be a better time? I'll keep this short either way."

If they're available:
> "I'm calling because I sent you a note about Chasing a Cure and our summer campaign — I wanted to make sure it didn't get lost in your inbox and to hear how you're doing."

Brief check-in (1–2 minutes, genuinely). Ask about their family, their work, whatever is natural. This is a relationship.

---

### The bridge (1 minute)

> "The reason I'm calling before our public launch is that I'm reaching out personally to the people who matter most to this foundation first. We're trying to raise $150,000 this summer for Parkinson's research through The Michael J. Fox Foundation — going directly to the science. Rob and I run this entirely on volunteer time — no salaries — and keep costs as lean as legally possible."

---

### The ask (30 seconds — say the number clearly, then stop)

> "I'd love to ask you to consider a gift of $[ASK AMOUNT]. That would go directly to MJFF and the research we're funding this summer."

**Then stop. Do not fill the silence.** Wait for their response.

---

### If they say yes:

> "That means more than you know. I'll send you the donation link today — it goes directly through our Team Fox page so the funds flow straight to MJFF. And I'll make sure you get something personal from us when we announce the total raised."

Lock down the details: Will they give online? Do they prefer to write a check? Do they want an invoice? Note it and follow up same day.

---

### If they ask questions:

**"Where exactly does the money go?"**
> "Your gift goes to The Michael J. Fox Foundation through our Team Fox fundraising page. MJFF is the world's largest private funder of Parkinson's research — 4-star rated on Charity Navigator. We're entirely volunteer-run and take no salaries, and we keep our operating costs to the minimum."

**"Can I think about it?"**
> "Of course. I'll send you our donation page and a quick summary. When would be a good time to reconnect — maybe [specific date, one week out]?"

**"What will the money fund specifically?"**
> "MJFF allocates unrestricted Team Fox gifts to their highest-priority research programs — currently focused on [insert current MJFF research focus area, e.g., biomarker development / alpha-synuclein research]. I can send you a link to their current research priorities if that would be helpful."

**"Will I be invited to Concert for a Cure?"**
> "Absolutely — I'd love for you to be there. It's July 18 at our lake home in Fishers. A gift at this level comes with a personal invitation from us."

---

### If they decline:

> "I completely understand, and I'm grateful you took the time to talk. Would you be open to coming to Concert for a Cure on July 18 as our guest? It would mean a lot to have you there."

Never pressure. A graceful exit keeps the relationship — and they may give at the event.

---

### Close

> "Thank you for your time. This means a lot — to Rob and me personally, and to the families who are waiting for this research to reach them. I'll be in touch."

## Thank-You Sequence (After a Gift Is Made)

### Touch 1 — Phone call (within 48 hours)

Call personally. Keep it short. The job is gratitude, not more asking.

> "Hi [First name], it's Wendy. I just wanted to call and say thank you personally — your gift means the world to us. Knowing that it goes directly to MJFF and the research makes it real. We'll make sure you're updated on what it funds. And we can't wait to see you on July 18."

---

### Touch 2 — Handwritten note (within 1 week)

Write it by hand. No template — use their name, reference their gift amount, say something specific.

Example structure:
- Sentence 1: Thank them by name and reference the specific gift
- Sentence 2: Connect their gift to Sue's story or the research
- Sentence 3: Confirm the Concert for a Cure invitation
- Sentence 4: Close with a personal line

Keep it to a notecard. Sincerity over length.

---

### Touch 3 — Impact update (within 30 days)

Send a brief email — not a template — with a link to a current MJFF research update.

> "[First name] — I wanted to share this with you. MJFF just announced [research milestone/update]. Your gift is part of what makes work like this possible. Thank you. We'll see you July 18. — Wendy"

## Objection Quick Reference

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I already give to MJFF directly." | "That's wonderful — your support for their research is exactly the right instinct. A gift to us flows through Team Fox directly to MJFF, so it compounds what you're already doing. And you'd be supporting the local community that's built around this mission." |
| "I'm not sure I can give at that level." | "Any amount you're comfortable with is meaningful — it goes directly to the research. Would a gift of $[lower amount] feel right?" |
| "I want to wait and give at the event." | "We'd love that — please come as our personal guests. The fund-a-need at the event is a real moment and your presence makes a difference. Let me put you on the list." |
| "I need to check with my spouse / partner." | "Of course. I'll send you the link and a summary you can share with them. Can I follow up in a few days?" |
| "What happens if you don't hit your goal?" | "Every dollar we raise gets transferred to MJFF regardless of whether we hit $150K. There's no minimum — the science benefits from every gift." |
