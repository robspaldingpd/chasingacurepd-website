Brand · Logo usage

Using the mark

The Chasing a Cure mark is a black "C" cradling a monarch butterfly inside a blush circle. It's hand‑drawn and asymmetric on purpose — keep it that way. The rules below are short. Read them anyway.

Anatomy

Three parts, one mark. Don't separate them outside of the cases listed under "marks" in the asset library.

  • The C — black, hand‑drawn, slightly open on the right. It's the wordmark anchor.
  • The butterfly — orange monarch, layered over the C. The motion symbol; metamorphosis, lift, hope.
  • The blush circle — the field. #E8C0C0, exactly. Hold space and warmth.

Minimum sizes

The mark needs room to breathe. Below 32px the butterfly disappears — use the simplified butterfly badge instead.

32px · favicon min

Clear space

Reserve a margin equal to ¼ the mark's diameter on every side. Nothing should enter that ring — not type, not photo edges, not other logos.

¼ × diameter

The orange dashed ring shows the no‑go zone. When placing the mark next to text or other content, use this ring as the closest the neighbor can sit.

  • Standard ratio of clearspace = 0.25 × logo width
  • For nav/header use, ring may shrink to 0.15× when space is tight
  • Never crop the blush circle — the mark always sits as a whole disc

Do

Do
On ink-900Footer, dark hero. Blush circle pops without losing the C.
Do
On photographyOK over photos when there's a calm/dark area. Add a soft drop‑shadow for legibility.

Don't

All eight are wrong. Each breaks the brand in a different way.

Don't
StretchThe mark is square. Always preserve aspect ratio.
Don't
Rotate or tiltThe C and butterfly read upright. Don't reframe.
Don't
RecolorThree colors only — black, monarch orange, blush pink. No exceptions.
Don't
Add effectsNo outer glows, hard shadows, embosses. The mark is flat.
Don't
Decorative gradientsThe blush field is the background. Don't put it on neon, gradient, or unrelated brand colors.
Don't
On orangeThe butterfly disappears. Reserve orange for CTAs, not as a logo background.
Don't
On busy photosIf the photo is high‑detail, switch to a card or use the white wordmark only.
Don't
Crop the circleThe blush disc is part of the mark. Always show it whole.
