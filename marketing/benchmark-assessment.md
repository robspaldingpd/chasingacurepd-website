## Overall Summary

Chasing a Cure PD is **above average for a founder-stage nonprofit** and has a credibility foundation most small organizations lack — a world-class research partner (MJFF/Team Fox), founders with genuine industry expertise, and a brand story that is specific and emotionally authentic. The messaging is already stronger than most organizations at this stage.

The gaps are not about mission or message — they are about **infrastructure, documentation, and systems**. The top 10% of healthcare nonprofits have built the back-office scaffolding that turns one-time donors into lifelong supporters and earns institutional trust. That is what needs to be built here.

## Where We Are Genuinely Strong

### 1. Messaging and brand story — top 10% quality
The three anchor lines (*Science fuels hope / We fund the science behind breakthroughs / Every dollar goes directly to research*), the founding story, and the tagline (*Built from love. Backed by science. Focused on the cure.*) are as good as anything in the Parkinson's nonprofit landscape. Most small nonprofits have vague, interchangeable mission statements. Ours is specific, emotionally coherent, and differentiated.

**Comparison:** The Parkinson's Foundation uses "every 85 cents to programs." The MJFF uses "world's largest nonprofit funder." We use a founding story and a 100% promise — that is a distinct lane and it works.

### 2. Research partner — best possible
Team Fox / MJFF is Platinum-rated on GuideStar, 4-star on Charity Navigator, and the world's most credible Parkinson's research funder. Being their fundraising partner gives us borrowed credibility that takes other organizations years to earn. Every donor who questions "where does the money go?" gets a world-class answer.

### 3. Fundraising efficiency — on track to be excellent
The campaign plan targets $150K gross on a $30K budget = $0.20 per dollar raised. That lands in the top tier ($0.15–$0.25) of the Charity Navigator benchmark. If the boat raffle and major gifts perform, the ratio could reach $0.15 or better.

### 4. 100% volunteer model — a genuine differentiator
Top-performing nonprofits average 85% program expense ratios. We operate at effectively 100% program allocation (excluding in-kind volunteer time). That is a real, documentable claim — but only if we can substantiate it with transparent financial reporting.

## Where We Need Work — Prioritized

### Priority 1: GuideStar / Candid Seal — fix in 2-4 weeks

**The gap:** We are likely unrated or Bronze-level. Organizations with any Candid Seal average **53% more contributions** than unsealed organizations. 77% of donations made through GuideStar go to Seal holders.

**The standard:** MJFF, Parkinson's Foundation, and APDA are all at Platinum or Gold.

**What to do:**
- Log in to candid.org/guidestar and complete the full organizational profile → immediate Bronze
- Upload Form 990 and financial data → Silver (1-2 weeks)
- Add program descriptions and outcome data → Gold (2-4 weeks)
- Add quantified impact metrics and MJFF transfer documentation → Platinum (ongoing)

**Estimated time:** 4-6 hours to reach Gold. No cost.

---

### Priority 2: Charity Navigator — start the clock now

**The gap:** Charity Navigator requires **3 years of Form 990 filings** before it can issue a full rating. If we have not submitted 990s consistently since 2018, we may not qualify yet. MJFF, APDA, and Parkinson's Foundation are all 4-star rated.

**What to do:**
- Confirm with accountant/treasurer that all 990s are filed and current
- Create a Charity Navigator Nonprofit Portal account and submit data proactively
- Begin tracking the four Encompass Beacon inputs now: Finance (program ratio, fundraising efficiency), Impact (outcomes), Leadership (board structure), and Culture (donor feedback)

**Timeline:** Cannot rush — the 3-year clock is fixed. But starting now compounds forward.

---

### Priority 3: Donor retention infrastructure — critical gap

**The gap:** Only **14-19% of first-time donors give a second time** across the sector. The top 10% target 40-50%+ retention by building a structured 90-day follow-up sequence. There is no documented retention sequence in our current materials.

**The 90-day sequence that top organizations use:**
| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Day 0 (same day) | Automated Zeffy receipt + personal thank-you email from Wendy |
| Day 3 | Handwritten note (or personal email) from Wendy or Rob |
| Day 30 | Impact story: "Here's what your gift is funding" — link to MJFF research update |
| Day 60 | Non-ask touchpoint: patient story, behind-the-scenes update |
| Day 90 | Second gift ask + upgrade to monthly giving |

**Phone outreach data:** Donors who receive a thank-you call within 90 days retain at **58% vs. 33%** without a call. That is a massive lift for a personal organization like ours — Wendy calling donors is a real competitive advantage.

**What to do:**
- Build this sequence in whatever email tool we use (Mailchimp, Constant Contact, or Zeffy)
- Script a 2-minute thank-you call that Wendy or Rob makes to every $100+ first-time donor
- Create a simple donor CRM (even a Google Sheet works to start) tracking each donor's last contact date, giving history, and follow-up status

---

### Priority 4: Website — fix the 404s and coming-soon pages

**The gap:** The Concert for a Cure page returns a 404. The 2026 Concert page shows "coming soon." With the campaign launching April 20, these pages need to be live and converting.

**Industry benchmark:** 0.16% of website visitors donate (sector average). Top performers hit 0.20-0.30%. Mobile responsiveness is required — 65%+ of nonprofit traffic is now mobile.

**What to do immediately:**
- Get the Concert for a Cure landing page live with Zeffy RSVP flow (this is Week 1 in the campaign plan — it should already be in progress)
- Audit all navigation links for 404s
- Add trust signals above the fold: EIN, GuideStar seal, MJFF partnership logo, "100% to research" badge
- Ensure the donate button is visible on every page, top-right on desktop

---

### Priority 5: Email marketing — establish baseline and segment

**The gap:** No documented email metrics. The sector average open rate is 28.59%; top performers hit 35-40%. Without baseline data, we cannot optimize.

**What to do:**
- Set up basic segmentation in the email tool: (1) donors, (2) prospects/newsletter, (3) event attendees, (4) lapsed donors
- Track open rate, click rate, and unsubscribe rate for every send starting now
- Build 3-4 reusable email templates: welcome/thank-you, impact story, event/campaign, year-end ask
- Target frequency: 2-3x/month for active donors; 1-2x/month for prospects

---

### Priority 6: Storytelling inventory — beyond Sue's story

**The gap:** Sue's story is powerful. But top 10% healthcare nonprofits document **12+ distinct stories per year** across patients, caregivers, researchers, and volunteers. A single story, no matter how strong, creates single-point-of-failure messaging.

**The standard:** Character → Conflict → Resolution → Outcome. The nonprofit is the guide, not the hero.

**What to do:**
- Identify 3-5 people in our network who live with Parkinson's or are caregivers and are willing to share their story
- Document Wendy's godfather's story and Rob's aunt's story (with permission) — they are already referenced but never featured
- Build a story bank: written, photo, and ideally short video (60 seconds) for each
- Schedule: 1 new story per month across email, social, and website

---

### Priority 7: Monthly giving program — build the recurring base

**The gap:** Top nonprofits generate 15-20%+ of online revenue from recurring monthly donors. Monthly donors have a **90%+ annual retention rate** vs. 31.9% for one-time donors. We have no documented monthly giving program.

**Why it matters for our math:** If we convert 50 one-time donors at $50/month, that is $30,000/year in recurring, predictable research funding with no additional campaign cost.

**What to do:**
- Create a named monthly giving program (e.g., *The Science Circle* or *Sue's Promise Fund*)
- Make monthly giving the default or prominently featured option on the donation page
- Add upgrade-to-monthly ask at the 90-day mark in the retention sequence
- Set a goal: 50+ monthly donors by year-end

---

### Priority 8: Social media — consistency over volume

**The gap:** Healthcare nonprofit benchmarks are 2-4% engagement rate, 3-4 posts/week. Without a documented content calendar or engagement tracking, we cannot know where we stand.

**Realistic target for a volunteer-run org:** 3 posts/week across Instagram and Facebook, 2/week on LinkedIn. Batch-create one month at a time.

**Content mix (use the five pillars from the messaging guide):** Mission/urgency (20%), the story (25%), science/research (20%), community (20%), events/action (15%).

## Bottom Line

Chasing a Cure PD has what money cannot buy and cannot be rushed: a true founding story, personal stakes, a world-class research partner, and founders with the credibility to earn trust in a room full of pharma executives or major donors.

What separates us from the top 10% is not mission quality — it is the infrastructure that converts first-time donors into loyal supporters, earns institutional trust signals (GuideStar, Charity Navigator, BBB), and gives volunteers a consistent playbook to execute without reinventing everything each cycle.

The good news: most of these gaps can be closed in 60-90 days with focused effort. None of them require money — they require documentation, process, and someone to own each system.
